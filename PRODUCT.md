# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two audiences sharing the same surface:

**Students / prospective learners** — someone deciding where to invest £500–2000 on an aesthetics training course. They arrive in research mode: comparing trainers, building trust, assessing credibility. They need to find the right person quickly and feel confident the directory has been curated. A single visit might mean the difference between booking and bouncing.

**Trainers (paying subscribers)** — aesthetics professionals on Standard (£150/mo) or Premium (£249/mo) plans who need their listing to perform. Their job is to present credentials compellingly and convert student attention into enquiries. Premium subscribers have paid meaningfully more and expect that investment to show.

## Product Purpose

A vetted public directory of UK aesthetics trainers. Students discover qualified professionals filtered by specialism and location; trainers gain a high-trust showcase for their expertise. Success looks like: a student finds a credible match in under 60 seconds, a trainer's Premium listing visibly justifies the price difference, and the directory feels authoritative enough that being listed is itself a signal of quality.

## Brand Personality

Trusted, Modern, Approachable.

The aesthetics industry sits between beauty and healthcare — it should feel neither like a candle-brand Instagram page nor a hospital brochure. The tone is a confident professional network: credible enough that a student trusts it with a significant purchase, warm enough that it doesn't intimidate someone new to the industry.

## Anti-references

- **Generic job-board gray** — the most important anti-reference. Total Jobs / Reed aesthetic: clinical, utilitarian, zero personality. This is exactly where the current design sits and what we're moving away from.
- No over-luxe beauty aesthetics (dusty rose, heavy script serif, Treatwell / Fresha energy).
- No medical sterility (hospital white, clinical blue, cold and impersonal).
- No generic SaaS-blue B2B defaults.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust before decoration.** Every visual choice must earn its place by reinforcing credibility. Decoration that doesn't signal quality should be cut.
2. **Premium must feel earned.** The visual difference between Standard and Premium listings must feel like a real quality signal — not a badge you add, but a presence upgrade that makes the price difference legible.
3. **Warm authority.** The industry is part beauty, part medical. The design lives in the space between the two: professional without being cold, inviting without being soft.
4. **Clarity under financial pressure.** Students are making significant spend decisions. Every label, filter, and data point must be instantly legible. Ambiguity costs conversions and trust.
5. **Modern but durable.** Restrained palette and solid typographic hierarchy over surface trends. A directory ages — the design shouldn't.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. 4.5:1 minimum contrast for body text, 3:1 for large/bold text. Full keyboard navigation. Semantic HTML. Reduced-motion alternatives for any animations.
